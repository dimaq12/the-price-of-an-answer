# The Price of an Answer

**Spectra Without Matrices · Journey II.** Part I — [Never Quantum at All](https://github.com/dimaq12/do-we-need-quantum-computing) — used one matrix-free number, the effective rank Φ₁, to tell genuine quantum speedups from costumes. This repository points the same dial at 35 famous nonlinear PDEs and asks every solver the rudest possible question: *what am I actually paying you for?*

The claim is not asserted — it is **run**. Three stands, every number printed against a hidden ground truth, all seeds deterministic (you will reproduce these exact tables).

## The stands

| Stand | What it demonstrates | Healthy verdict |
|---|---|---|
| [`stands/seven_solves.py`](stands/seven_solves.py) | ~7 solves buy a Chebyshev surrogate answering u(T;k) for any k in a ±35% range — plus the Φ₁ dial, Fisher information, and zero-solve ML inversion | forward 34/35 at ~5·10⁻¹⁵, ~570× faster queries; inverse 35/35 < 10⁻¹⁰; the NLS soliton is the honest wall, and Φ₁ ≈ 2.7 flags it in advance |
| [`stands/crb_optimal.py`](stands/crb_optimal.py) | parameter recovery on the raw solver that **saturates the Cramér–Rao bound** — the information-theoretic floor no estimator can beat | clean 35/35 < 10⁻¹⁰; under noise, median \|Δk\|/CRB ≈ 0.6–0.9 vs the theoretical 0.674 of an optimal estimator |
| [`stands/one_snapshot_map.py`](stands/one_snapshot_map.py) | a whole viscosity map ν(x) from ONE noisy snapshot — with the blind zones **measured**, not hallucinated | ~10 of 128 directions visible at σ=1%; defect localized; ~99% of the truth inside the stated 2σ bars; at σ=5% the stand honestly reports the snapshot contains *nothing* |

Shared machinery lives in [`stands/pde_zoo.py`](stands/pde_zoo.py): the 35 equations (Burgers, KdV, Kuramoto–Sivashinsky chaos, NLS soliton, Camassa–Holm, fractional heat, …), the spectral Strang solver, and the black-box observable interface the stands probe — like a lab instrument, never looking inside.

## The honest limits (printed by the stands, not hidden in a footnote)

- The surrogate is built per initial condition and time horizon; test horizons are short. It is a cache around a question family, not a general solver replacement.
- The **NLS bright soliton** resists: even 63 nodes / 200 solves leave ~10⁻² forward error — a Kolmogorov n-width wall (breathing profiles do not compress into linear bases). The dial predicted it: Φ₁ = 2.7, the only reading far from 1.00 in the zoo. Its parameter still inverts to the exact bit, through the amplitude.
- Clean-data inverse targets are generated by the same discretization (no model error). Under noise the comparison is against the Cramér–Rao bound, which is the honest scale.
- The components — Chebyshev interpolation, sensitivity analysis, maximum likelihood — are classical. Nothing here is new mathematics; what is new is the discipline of *measuring the structure first and pricing the answer accordingly*.
